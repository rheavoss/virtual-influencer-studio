## 2. The Loopholes and Critical Flags

### LOOPHOLE A: Buying Aged Instagram Accounts — HIGH RISK for Us

- **His Claim:** "Aged accounts work better. New Instagram accounts now are likely to get banned, especially if a beginner. I recommend 1+ year aged accounts from a Telegram seller for ~$6."
- **The Loophole:** He is a more aggressive, higher-risk operator than Renato. Buying an aged account creates a specific risk profile that is dangerous in our context:
  1. The account's historical activity, device history, and IP associations are unknown. If the previous owner violated TOS, that history is attached to the account.
  2. The transfer of account ownership (new device + new IP) triggers Instagram's security system, always requiring phone number verification. This creates the exact "suspicious login" event that Renato warned about.
  3. $6 Telegram-sourced aged accounts are typically compromised, resold, or acquired via bot farms — Instagram's detection systems flag these accounts as high-risk from acquisition.
- **Our Position:** We build from scratch. The 72-hour warmup protocol combined with a US-proxied device will establish a clean, high-quality trust signal. An organic account with pristine history is worth more long-term than a contaminated aged account.

### LOOPHOLE B: "Celebrity Farming" Strategy — Legal and Platform Risk

- **His Claim:** His current viral strategy is editing your AI model into images with celebrities (Ronaldo, Trump, Maduro) and posting them as if they are real. He shows an example getting 1.6 million likes.
- **The Loophole — THREE reasons this is a trap:**
  1. **Instagram Removal Risk:** Meta's automated systems use facial recognition to detect unauthorized use of verified public figures' likenesses. Celebrity images trigger priority content review queues. The Ronaldo example he showed likely survived because of luck, not because it is safe.
  2. **Legal Liability:** Using a celebrity's likeness commercially (to drive subscriptions to a paid platform) without consent is a clear violation of Right of Publicity laws in the USA, UK, and India. This is not a TOS violation — this is a civil lawsuit exposure vector. Nicholas Maduro is particularly dangerous given US political sensitivities around his image.
  3. **Platform Policy:** Instagram's Community Standards explicitly prohibit "manipulated media" that misrepresents real people. This is entirely separate from the NSFW content rules.
- **Our Action:** Do NOT use celebrity farming. Jasmine's viral hooks (Saree Transition, paparazzi street shots, in-car GFE) are designed to go viral on their own merits without the legal exposure.

### LOOPHOLE C: "3 Accounts Per Phone" — Contradicts Our OPSEC

- **His Claim:** "Anything under 3 Instagram pages per phone."
- **Renato's Claim:** "1 big account per phone to avoid chain bans."
- **The Contradiction:** Oscar is an agency operator treating accounts as disposable inventory. We are building a long-term brand asset. His risk tolerance is completely different.
- **Our Position:** Jasmine gets one isolated device. No other accounts on that phone, ever.

### LOOPHOLE D: The OnlyFans Pivot — Why He Uses It AND Why We Don't

- **His Claim:** He implies he found "ways around" getting AI models onto OnlyFans despite the platform's policy against AI-generated content.
- **The Loophole:** OnlyFans' Terms of Service explicitly require creators to verify their own identity with government ID. Using an AI model violates this by definition. He is operating in a gray-to-black area that has a clearly defined termination risk.
- **Our Position:** We already made this decision correctly. Fanvue is purpose-built for AI creators and explicitly permits AI-generated content. The $37K he made in 45 days on OnlyFans is real revenue - but it exists on a platform where his account can be terminated with no appeal at any moment.
